# Business Insights

# Customer Churn Analysis

---

## Objective

The objective of this analysis was to identify the major factors contributing to customer churn and provide meaningful insights to support business decision-making.

---

## Key Business Insights

### 1. Customer Churn Rate

The analysis identified a significant number of customers who have churned, indicating that customer retention is a key business challenge.

**Business Impact**
- High churn reduces recurring revenue.
- Increased customer acquisition costs.
- Lower customer lifetime value.

---

### 2. Contract Type Influences Churn

Customers with **Month-to-Month** contracts have the highest churn compared to customers with One-Year and Two-Year contracts.

**Business Impact**
- Long-term contracts improve customer retention.
- Short-term customers require targeted retention strategies.

---

### 3. Customer Tenure Affects Retention

Customers with shorter tenure are more likely to leave the company.

**Business Impact**
- Early customer engagement is critical.
- Strong onboarding can improve long-term retention.

---

### 4. Monthly Charges and Churn

Customers with higher monthly charges show a greater likelihood of churning.

**Business Impact**
- Pricing plays an important role in customer satisfaction.
- Competitive pricing and bundled services may reduce churn.

---

### 5. Customer Satisfaction

Customers with lower satisfaction scores are more likely to churn.

**Business Impact**
- Improving customer support and service quality can increase retention.

---

### 6. Internet Services

Customer churn varies across different internet service types.

**Business Impact**
- Certain service offerings require quality improvements and better customer support.

---

### 7. Payment Method

Some payment methods are associated with higher churn.

**Business Impact**
- Encouraging automated payment methods may improve customer retention.

---

### 8. High-Value Customers

Customers with higher Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV) contribute significantly to total revenue.

**Business Impact**
- Retaining high-value customers should be a strategic priority.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis shows that customer churn is strongly influenced by contract type, tenure, monthly charges, customer satisfaction, and service usage. These insights provide valuable guidance for improving customer retention and increasing business revenue.
