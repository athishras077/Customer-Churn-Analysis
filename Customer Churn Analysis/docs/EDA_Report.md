# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Report

# Customer Churn Analysis

---

## Objective

The objective of Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is to understand the dataset, identify trends and patterns, analyze customer behavior, and discover the factors influencing customer churn.

---

## Analysis Performed

### Univariate Analysis

- Customer Churn Distribution
- Gender Distribution
- Contract Distribution
- Internet Type Distribution
- Payment Method Distribution
- Monthly Charge Distribution
- Tenure Distribution

---

### Bivariate Analysis

- Gender vs Churn
- Senior Citizen vs Churn
- Contract vs Churn
- Internet Type vs Churn
- Payment Method vs Churn
- Monthly Charge vs Churn
- Tenure vs Churn
- Satisfaction Score vs Churn

---

### Multivariate Analysis

- Contract × Internet Type × Churn
- Customer Status × Contract
- Monthly Charge by Contract
- Total Revenue by Customer Status

---

### Correlation Analysis

- Correlation Matrix
- Relationship among numerical variables
- Identification of highly correlated features

---

## Key Findings

- Overall customer churn rate was analyzed.
- Customers with shorter contracts showed higher churn.
- Monthly contracts experienced more churn than long-term contracts.
- Customers with lower tenure were more likely to churn.
- Satisfaction score showed a strong relationship with churn.
- Monthly charges varied across customer segments.
- Customer behavior differed across internet service and payment methods.

---

## Conclusion

Exploratory Data Analysis successfully identified the major patterns and relationships within the dataset. These findings will support SQL analysis, KPI development, dashboard creation, and business recommendations.
